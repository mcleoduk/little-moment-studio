How Far in Advance Should You Book Birthday Balloons?

Little Moment Studio
June 2026
10 min read

Planning a birthday party is much easier when the main details are booked early — and balloons are one of the things that can make the biggest difference to the look of the celebration. Whether you are planning a children’s birthday, first birthday, baby shower or larger party setup, booking your balloons at the right time helps with colour choice, design planning, setup timing and availability.

At Little Moment Studio, we create balloon displays and styled celebration setups for birthdays across Kent and the surrounding areas. This guide explains when to book, why early planning helps and what details are useful to have ready before you enquire.

Why Booking Early Helps

Balloon displays are not just pick-up-and-place decorations. A good setup needs planning, preparation and enough time to make sure the colours, shape and style suit the party. Booking early gives you more choice and less stress.

It helps with:

  • securing your preferred date
  • choosing the right balloon colours
  • planning the size and style of the display
  • matching balloons to the cake or theme
  • checking venue access and setup times
  • coordinating with other suppliers
  • avoiding last-minute compromises

The earlier you book, the more time there is to get the colour palette, size and styling exactly right.

When to Book First Birthday Balloons

For a first birthday, book 4–6 weeks in advance. First birthdays often involve more planning because families want the display to feel extra special for photos. The setup may include:

  • number one balloon
  • balloon garland
  • backdrop
  • cake table styling
  • soft colour palette
  • dessert table
  • personalised sign
  • theme details

Popular first birthday themes such as teddy bear, farm, safari, princess, unicorn or football often need more design time. If you are planning a venue party, booking early also helps coordinate access times and setup requirements.

When to Book a Balloon Garland

For a balloon garland, aim to book 2–4 weeks in advance. A simple garland may be possible with less notice, but a styled garland with specific colours, backdrop styling or table coordination is better booked earlier.

Book earlier if you want:

  • custom or specific colours
  • a large garland
  • a backdrop included
  • a dessert table setup
  • a venue installation
  • a weekend date
  • a themed design

A Garland Is Often the Main Feature

A balloon garland is usually the most visible part of the party setup. Giving enough notice makes it easier to get the colour balance, size and shape exactly right — and leaves time for any adjustments before the day.

When to Book Baby Shower Balloons

For baby shower balloons, book 4–6 weeks in advance where possible. Baby showers often have softer, more specific colour palettes that need careful coordination with the cake, welcome sign, flowers and venue styling.

Baby showers are also usually held on weekends, so earlier booking helps secure your preferred date and time.

Do Weekend Bookings Fill Up Faster?

Yes. Most birthday parties, baby showers and children’s parties take place on Saturdays or Sundays, so weekend dates tend to fill fastest — especially during spring, summer and school holiday periods.

Can You Book Last Minute?

Sometimes, yes. Last-minute bookings may be possible depending on date availability, setup location, colour availability and the size and complexity of the display.

However, last-minute bookings may have fewer options. You may need to be flexible on colours, style, setup time or size. If your party is soon, it is still worth enquiring — a simpler display may still be possible.

Always Worth Asking

Even if your party is very soon, send an enquiry. We will always let you know honestly what is possible. A simpler display at short notice is often better than no display at all.

Should Balloons Match the Cake and Dessert Table?

They do not need to match exactly, but they should feel connected. Repeating two or three colours across the balloons, cake and table details makes the whole party setup feel cohesive without requiring everything to be identical. Seasonal Dates and School Holidays

Seasonal dates and school holidays can book up faster than usual. For Easter parties, summer birthdays, Halloween, Christmas events, bank holiday weekends and school holiday birthdays, booking 6–8 weeks in advance is sensible — especially for larger displays or specific themes.

Your Questions Answered

How far in advance should I book birthday balloons?

For most birthday balloon displays, book 2–4 weeks in advance. For weekends, first birthdays, baby showers or larger installations, 4–6 weeks is safer. For seasonal dates or school holidays, aim for 6–8 weeks ahead.

Can I book balloons at the last minute?

Sometimes, yes. Last-minute bookings may be possible depending on availability, location, colour choice and setup size. Simpler displays are more likely to be possible at short notice. It is always worth enquiring even if your party is soon.

How early should I book a balloon garland?

A balloon garland is best booked 2–4 weeks before the party. For larger garlands, weekend parties or displays with a backdrop, book 4–6 weeks ahead if possible.

When should I book balloons for a first birthday?

For a first birthday, book 4–6 weeks in advance. First birthday setups are often more detailed and may include a backdrop, number balloon, cake table styling and themed colours.

Do weekend balloon bookings fill up faster?

Yes. Saturdays and Sundays are the most popular party days, so weekend dates usually fill faster than weekdays. Aim for 4 weeks ahead for a standard display, and 4–6 weeks for first birthdays, baby showers or larger setups.

Can I choose balloon colours after booking?

Usually, yes. Many customers secure the date first and finalise colours once they have chosen a cake, theme or venue decor. If you want very specific colours, it is better to confirm them early as some shades may need to be sourced in advance.

Do I need to know my venue before booking?

It helps, but you can still enquire before the venue is fully confirmed. The final design may depend on access times, room size and whether the setup is indoor or outdoor. Once the venue is confirmed, the balloon design can be planned more accurately.

What information should I send when enquiring?

Send the party date, location, rough time, theme, colour ideas, whether the setup is indoor or outdoor, and any inspiration photos you like. You do not need to have every detail finalised — sharing what you know is enough to start the conversation.
