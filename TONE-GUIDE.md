# Tone & Voice Guide — Little Moment Studio

## Who We Are

Little Moment Studio is a celebration styling studio based in Sittingbourne, Kent. We create beautiful balloon displays for baby showers, birthdays, gender reveals and special occasions. We position ourselves as mid-to-premium — not a budget party shop, not intimidating luxury. The sweet spot between the two.

## Brand Voice

**If Little Moment Studio was a person at a party, they'd be:** the friendly, stylish one who makes everyone feel welcome. Not the loudest in the room, not showing off — just genuinely warm, with great taste, and happy to share ideas without making anyone feel silly for asking.

### Voice Pillars

1. **Warm but not gushy** — We're friendly and approachable, never cold or corporate. But we don't overdo it with exclamation marks, emojis in copy, or forced enthusiasm.
2. **Knowledgeable but not preachy** — We know our craft and share useful information generously. We never talk down to people or make them feel they should already know something.
3. **Honest but not blunt** — We give real, practical advice (including budget realities). We acknowledge when something is a matter of taste rather than pretending there's only one right answer.
4. **Elevated but not exclusive** — Our work is premium quality, but our tone never makes people feel priced out or unwelcome. We style celebrations, not status symbols.

## Language Rules

### Always
- **British English** throughout (colour, organised, centre, favourite)
- **Prices in £** with UK-relevant ranges
- **UK references** — Hobbycraft not Michaels, garden not backyard, crisps not chips, boot of the car not trunk
- **"We" not "I"** — future-proofs for team growth and sounds more established
- **Active voice** — "We style your celebration" not "Your celebration will be styled by us"
- **Plain language** — say "choose" not "select", "use" not "utilise", "help" not "facilitate"

### Never
- **Don't state how long we've been going** — let the work speak for itself
- **No Pinterest-speak** — avoid "obsessed!", "I'm dying!", "literally the cutest thing ever", "I can't even"
- **No hard sell** — articles inform and inspire, they don't push bookings. The CTA is always soft ("get in touch", "we'd love to help")
- **No jargon without explaining it** — if we mention "organic garland" or "double-stuffed balloons", briefly explain what that means
- **No American spellings or references** — this is a UK business serving Kent families
- **No superlatives we can't back up** — avoid "the best in Kent", "the most amazing". Let clients decide that
- **No talking down about DIY or budget options** — many of our clients start with DIY. We respect that and position professional styling as the next step, not a correction

## Writing Style

### Sentence & Paragraph Structure
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences max)
- Mix sentence lengths — a short punchy line after a longer one creates rhythm
- Lead with the useful information, not the preamble
- Use dashes (—) for asides rather than brackets where possible

### Headlines & Titles
- Playfair Display font, so titles should feel editorial not clickbait
- Use colons or ampersands to structure: "Baby Shower Balloon Ideas: Themes, Colours & Styling Tips"
- One word in italic (em tag) for visual accent — usually the emotional word: "Your Questions *Answered*", "Parties & *Egg Hunts*"
- Never use numbers-first clickbait: no "7 Amazing Ways to..." or "You Won't Believe..."

### Article Introductions
- Open with something the reader is already thinking or feeling
- Acknowledge the real question behind the search (they're not just looking for "balloon ideas" — they want their event to look amazing without the stress)
- Keep intros to 2-3 short paragraphs before the first subheading

### Tone Examples

**Too cold / corporate:**
> "Little Moment Studio provides premium balloon styling services for clients across the Kent region. Our portfolio demonstrates our commitment to excellence."

**Too casual / gushy:**
> "OMG we are SO excited to share our fave Easter balloon ideas with you!! These are literally gorgeous and we can't wait for you to see them! 🐣🎈"

**Too preachy / superior:**
> "Most people make the mistake of choosing the wrong balloon colours. If you want your event to actually look good, you need to understand colour theory."

**Just right (our voice):**
> "Choosing balloon colours shouldn't feel stressful — but with so many options, it's easy to overthink it. Here are the palettes we keep coming back to, and why they work so well together."

**Just right (addressing budget):**
> "A professional balloon garland typically starts from around £150 in Kent. That might sound like a lot for balloons — but when you factor in the hours of inflating, sizing and shaping, plus the cleanup afterwards, most clients tell us it was the best money they spent on the whole event."

**Just right (being honest):**
> "Let's be real — not every venue suits a huge balloon arch. Sometimes a smaller arrangement on the cake table or a cluster by the entrance makes more impact than trying to fill a massive space."

## Content Structure Patterns

### Q&A / Advice Articles
- Use the question as the subheading (exactly how someone would Google it)
- Answer the question in the first sentence, then expand
- Colour-coded category labels where relevant (etiquette, planning, budget, practical)
- Include a "what we'd suggest" line that gives our professional take without being pushy

### Seasonal Articles
- Publish 1-2 weeks before the event/holiday
- Lead with colour palettes (people search for these first)
- Include both DIY and professional options
- Mention Kent weather/practicalities where relevant (outdoor events)
- Acknowledge that not everyone celebrates the same way

### DIY / Tutorial Articles
- Materials list with UK suppliers and approximate prices in £
- Numbered steps with clear, achievable instructions
- Honest about difficulty level and time needed
- Never make DIY sound inferior to professional — position as different, not worse
- Suggest when professional help genuinely makes sense (e.g. large-scale installations)

### Trend Articles
- Reference real trend sources (Pantone, industry shows) not just "we think"
- Show how trends apply to real celebrations, not just styled photoshoots
- Include colour swatches/visual references
- Acknowledge that trends are suggestions, not rules

## SEO & Search Voice

- Write for the person searching, not the search engine
- Use natural keyword phrases in subheadings ("baby shower balloon ideas" not "balloon ideas baby shower optimal")
- Answer questions directly — this is what Google and AI search surfaces
- Include location naturally: "across Kent", "here in Sittingbourne", "our Kent clients"
- Every article should include FAQPage, HowTo, or BlogPosting schema

## Internal Linking

- Every article links to at least 2 other pages/articles
- Links should feel natural, not forced — "if you're planning a baby shower, our [complete Q&A guide] covers everything from who hosts to what it costs"
- Always link to relevant service pages where appropriate
- End-of-article CTA links to contact section, never feels like a hard sell
