Arrangement Tip

Start with the eucalyptus to set the shape and height. Add peonies as anchor points at varying heights. Fill with roses and ranunculus. Step back and check from 2 metres — that's the viewing distance at an event. Stems should cross inside the vase for stability; no stem should sit perfectly upright.

Little Moment Studio Section 2 — Balloon Garland Florals
Adding Florals to Installations

Flowers Within Balloon Garlands

Tucking silk flowers and foliage into a balloon garland elevates it from a standard build to a premium, styled installation. This is one of the defining features of our Luxe setups. The flowers sit between and behind the balloons — they should feel like they are growing out of the garland naturally, not placed on top.

Key Principle

Use heads and short stems only in garlands — not full-length stems. Full stems add unnecessary weight, which can pull balloons out of position. Cut or use pre-cut head-only products. Push stems into the knot clusters between balloon groups.

Placement Tips

Cluster, don't scatter. Group flowers together at 2–3 points along the garland (e.g. at the arch peak and two thirds of the way down each side) rather than spacing them evenly. Clustered florals photograph far better and look more intentional.

Face flowers outward. Every head should face the camera direction, not upward or sideways. Check from the front before photographing.

Hide the stems. No white plastic stems should be visible. Push fully into the balloon cluster or wrap with a small piece of eucalyptus leaf.
