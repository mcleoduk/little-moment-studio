04   Logo

Logo Usage

The logo is the single most consistent brand element. Never alter it — only change the background it sits on.

On Light
Use on cream (#FAF7F2) or white backgrounds
On Dark
Use on charcoal (#4A4A4A) backgrounds

✓ Always

  • Use the original file — never re-draw or recreate
  • Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the wordmark
  • Use on a solid, appropriate background colour only
  • Scale proportionally — never stretch or compress
  • Use the full name "Little Moment Studio" — never abbreviate to LMS

✕ Never

  • Never place on a busy photographic background
  • Never change the logo colours or add effects
  • Never rotate or distort the logo
  • Never place the logo at a size where it becomes unreadable
  • Never use "LMS", "Little Moments" or any abbreviation
Full business name: Little Moment Studio  ·  Never: "LMS", "Little Moments", "Little Moment's", "The Little Moment Studio"

Our voice is consistent across every touchpoint — website, social, captions, emails, packaging.

The Four Pillars
01
Warm but not gushy
Friendly and approachable, never cold. But no exclamation marks, emojis in copy, or forced enthusiasm.
02
Knowledgeable but not preachy
We share useful information generously. We never talk down or make people feel they should already know something.
03
Honest but not blunt
We give real advice including budget realities. We acknowledge when something is a matter of taste.

Elevated but not exclusive
Our work is premium quality but our tone never makes people feel priced out or unwelcome.

06   Language Rules

What We Say & How We Say It

Specific rules that keep our voice consistent across every piece of content we produce.

Always
  • British English — colour, organised, centre, favourite
  • Use "we" not "I" — future-proofs for growth
  • Active voice — "We style your celebration"
  • Prices in £ with UK-relevant ranges
  • Short paragraphs, 2–4 sentences max
  • Explain jargon — "organic garland (one made without a frame)"
  • Soft CTAs — "get in touch", "we'd love to help"
  • UK references — Hobbycraft, garden, boot of the car
Never
  • Pinterest-speak — "obsessed!", "literally the cutest"
  • Hard sell — never push bookings from editorial content
  • American spellings or cultural references
  • Superlatives — "the best in Kent", "most amazing"
  • Mention how long we've been going
  • Talk down about DIY or budget options
  • Abbreviate the business name to "LMS"
  • State "I" — always "we"

07   Photography

Photography Style

Our images should feel like a beautiful real celebration — not a staged photoshoot. Warm, genuine and aspirational without being unattainable.

☀️

Natural Light

Always prefer natural or soft diffused light. No harsh flash. Shoot toward or across windows. Golden hour light is ideal — it naturally matches our warm palette.

🎈

Show the Display, Not the Room

The balloon display should be the subject. Keep backgrounds clean and uncluttered. Move anything that doesn't add to the image before shooting.

🔍

Detail Shots

Get close. Balloon textures, colour combinations, florals, cake tables and styling details all photograph beautifully close-up. These are our strongest social images.

📐

Wide Establishing Shots

Always take at least one wide shot showing the full display in context. This is the hero image for the website and journal articles.

🎭

Real Moments

Candid shots of people enjoying the celebration are valuable — especially children. Always get consent. Never use overly staged or stiff poses.

📱

Phone Photography

Portrait mode for close-ups, standard for wide shots. Clean the lens. Avoid digital zoom. Edit toward warmth — not cool/blue. Lightroom presets preferred over in-app filters.

✓ Image Checklist
  • ✓ Clean background, no clutter
  • ✓ Balloon colours look accurate (white balance correct)
  • ✓ Display is in focus, not the background
  • ✓ Minimum 1200px wide for web use
  • ✓ Warm, not cool — adjust before posting
  • ✓ Saved as WebP for website use
✕ Avoid
  • ✕ Harsh overhead or flash lighting
  • ✕ Busy, distracting backgrounds
  • ✕ Over-filtered or heavily edited images
  • ✕ Blue-toned or cool images (off-brand)
  • ✕ Blurry or dark images, even if the moment was good
  • ✕ Images that show mess, unfinished work or equipment

08   Social Media

Social Media Guidelines

Our social presence should feel like a natural extension of the website — warm, considered and consistent.

Instagram

Primary channel. Focus on finished displays, detail shots and behind-the-scenes. Stories for process. Reels for reveals and transformations. Grid should look cohesive — warm tones throughout.

Facebook

Secondary channel. Repurpose Instagram content. Good for local community groups and sharing journal articles. Slightly longer captions work here — the audience is more patient.

Google Business

Post monthly updates and every new display. Reply to all reviews within 24 hours. Keep contact details and opening hours current. This directly affects local search ranking.

Caption Guidelines
Caption Do's
  • Lead with what makes this display interesting
  • Mention the occasion and colour palette
  • Include a soft call to action ("enquiries open")
  • Tag the venue if they're on Instagram
  • Use 5–10 relevant hashtags in the first comment
  • Write in the same warm, honest voice as the website
Caption Don'ts
  • Don't start with "We are so excited to share…"
  • Don't use strings of emojis in place of words
  • Don't use aggressive hashtag blocks (looks spammy)
  • Don't tag competitors or copy their caption style
  • Don't post without checking the image is actually sharp
  • Don't post more than twice daily (quality over quantity)

09   Brand Boundaries

What We Are Not

Knowing what the brand is not is just as important as knowing what it is. These boundaries keep every decision consistent.

A budget party shop

We are not competing on price. We don't communicate value through discounts, sales or cheapness. Our quality and care justify the price — we explain that, we don't apologise for it.

An intimidating luxury brand

We are not cold, aspirational or hard to approach. Our work is beautiful but our clients should never feel out of their depth talking to us. Warmth is non-negotiable.

An influencer brand

We are not trend-chasing or performance-driven. We don't use "obsessed", viral sounds or forced content. Everything we post is considered, not reactive.

A one-size-fits-all service

We are not a catalogue. Every display is personal. We don't sell packages like a menu — we collaborate on what works for each client's space, budget and vision.

An American brand

We are resolutely British and local. No American spellings, no US cultural references, no "y'all" or "fall". We are rooted in Sittingbourne, serving Kent families.

A party planner

We style balloon displays and dessert tables. We are not a full-service event planner. Being clear about what we do — and don't do — builds trust and sets proper expectations.

The brand in one sentence
Little Moment Studio creates beautiful, personal balloon displays for families celebrating the moments that matter — with warmth, craft and genuine care for how the day looks and feels.
